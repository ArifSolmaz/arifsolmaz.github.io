Research

My research interests revolve around exoplanets, stellar activity, and time-series analysis. I work with a strong background in data analysis and scientific computing.

Key Research Topics

1. Transiting Exoplanets Around Active Stars

  • Analysis of light curves for systems showing strong starspot activity (e.g., WASP-52, HAT-P-36, HAT-P-11, HATS-2).
  • Modeling the impact of occulted and unocculted spots on measured planetary parameters during transit.
  • Investigating the effects of stellar activity on Transit Timing Variations (TTV), transmission spectra, and planetary atmosphere interpretations.

2. Transmission Spectroscopy & Atmospheric Inference

  • Analysis of space telescope data (TESS, HST, JWST, CHEOPS).
  • Investigating the potential of stellar spots and faculae to mimic or bias molecular detections (e.g., TiO, silicate clouds).
  • Developing end-to-end data processing pipelines for reproducible light curve and spectral analysis.

3. Time Series Analysis & Machine Learning

  • Period searching, detrending, and noise modeling for space telescope photometry.
  • Exploring machine learning applications on variable star and exoplanet light curves.
  • Creating open-source tools for teaching and research (TESS/JWST analysis notebooks, Streamlit interfaces, etc.).

Ongoing / Planned Work

  • Development of infrastructure for spot-sensitive transit modeling (Python/Streamlit).
  • Projects for undergraduate students:
    • TESS light curve analysis.
    • Basic exoplanet discovery and characterization.
    • Projects combining robotics and astronomy (telescope control, simulations, etc.).
