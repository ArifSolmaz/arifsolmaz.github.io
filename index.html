Research Focus

My work sits at the intersection of stellar activity and exoplanet characterization. I ask: ‚ÄúHow does the star affect what we think we know about the planet?‚Äù I often work with space-based light curves from missions such as TESS, Kepler, and JWST.

Exoplanets & Stellar Activity

Many of the most interesting planets orbit magnetically active stars. Starspots and faculae distort transit light curves, biasing radius and timing measurements. I model these distortions to recover accurate planetary parameters and understand the impact on transit timing variations (TTVs).

Starspots & Stellar Rotation

I treat stellar ‚Äúnoise‚Äù as a valuable signal. By analyzing out-of-transit variability, I investigate starspot distributions, differential rotation, and the long-term magnetic evolution of host stars that shape our interpretation of exoplanet atmospheres.

Methods & Tools

I prefer pragmatic, physically motivated models: just complex enough to answer the scientific question. My work often combines Bayesian inference, MCMC, and forward modeling with open-source tools in Python to quantify uncertainties honestly and reproducibly.

For Students

My courses and labs emphasize problem solving with real datasets, including TESS light curves and other astrophysical time series. The goal is to move students from simply ‚Äúfollowing the recipe‚Äù to understanding the physical assumptions and limitations behind each model.

Project Philosophy

Good student projects are focused and finite. I value readable, well-documented code, clear visualizations, and honest error bars more than overly complex black-box approaches. Whenever possible, the results are made reproducible and shareable.
